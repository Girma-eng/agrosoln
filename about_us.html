The Context

The Challenge

Our current food system is broken - the way we produce, distribute and consume food isn't working effectively.

Stem from deep, interconnected issues: historical, cultural, and global, contributing to environmental degradation, inequality, and food insecurity, failing both people and the planet.

By focusing on short-term gains rather than long-term sustainability, this approach results in soil depletion, biodiversity loss, and increased climate vulnerability.

The Gaps

Mainly face three intertwined challenges that must be addressed to unlock their potential.

⏱️

Productivity Gap

Current output falls 30-50% below potential due to input limitations, limited mechanization and suboptimal farming practices.

🚚

Supply Gap

Challenges accessing inputs and reliable markets.

🎓

Skills Gap

Only 18% of farmers receive training, limiting adoption of modern techniques.

What We Strive For?

We envision a world where rural communities achieve true food sovereignty and independence by fully transitioning to regenerative lifestyles and systems - ensuring their resilience and empowering them to prosper for generations to come.

    • About Us

    Farm Hub Ethiopia (https://www.farmlab-ethiopia.com/) is an agricultural company established in 2023 with a core focus on improving crop production practices and empowering smallholder farmers in Ethiopia. With a commitment to sustainability, innovation, and social impact, we have been actively engaged in providing agricultural solutions and services tailored to the needs of smallholder farmers. As the hands-on implementation partner in the agricultural ecosystem, we translate innovations from research labs (like Mission Labs) into practical, farmer-ready solutions.

    Our Role in the System:

    ⚙️
    We are the implementation engine—delivering tools, training, and technologies directly to farmers' fields.

    Challenges Farm Hub Addresses

    🔍

    Low Mechanization

    Less than 1% of Ethiopian farms use tractors, with 85% of land tilled by hand, severely limiting productivity.

    ⛓️

    Fragmented Supply Chains

    Critical inputs and equipment often don't reach smallholder farmers due to weak distribution networks and high logistics costs.

    📚

    Knowledge Gaps

    Lack of access to training on machinery use and maintenance, reducing adoption rates. Lack of knowledge on agronomic practices and sustainability.

    📊

    The Mechanization Imperative

    Ethiopia's farm power availability is just 0.05 kW/ha (vs. 0.5+ kW/ha in emerging economies). Farm Hub's short-term focus on scalable mechanization solutions (pay-per-use models) can immediately boost productivity by 3-5x while solving affordability barriers.

    Our Vision

    Our vision is to revolutionize the agricultural sector by empowering farmers with innovative agricultural machinery, inputs, and sustainable practices, creating a future where Ethiopian agriculture thrives.

    Our Mission

    Through leveraging education, technology transfer, innovation and leadership development - our mission is to create a ripple effect of positive change, enabling rural communities to not only adapt but to lead in shaping a sustainable and resilient future.

    A one-stop-shop model connecting farmers to technologies, inputs and knowledge through an integrated network of Farm Hubs - the visible nodes of our transformative system - ensuring local scalability by embedding solutions within existing farmer networks and landscapes.

    Our ecosystem links Mission Labs (R&D), Farm Hubs (Delivery) and Regen Farms (Adoption) in continuous feedback cycles that refine solutions to local contexts.

    3

    4

    Systemic Impact

    By combining technology transfer, collective bargaining and market access, we create a virtuous cycle that boosts yields, livelihoods and food sovereignty across Africa.

    Our Achievements

    Farm Lab Ethiopia is committed to bridging the gap between traditional smallholder farming and modern agricultural solutions by promoting regenerative practices and cutting-edge technology. With a strong focus on innovation and sustainability, we have made notable progress.

    Learning Visits

    During our learning journey, we had the privilege of visiting UWEPO farm in Uganda to immerse ourselves in the world of regenerative agriculture. Owned by Nick Dekoning, the farm exemplifies excellence in sustainable farming practices.

    Visit to Wondo Genet College and Wondo Genet Research Center, accompanied by our partner from Studio Soal, Ireland, we had the opportunity to explore agroforestry and herbal plant production.

    Understanding the Existing System

    The survey aimed to understand the problem—the existing mechanization system and gap analysis by assessing existing equipment, infrastructure, personnel, business models, and training facilities of mechanization service providers in different parts of the country.

    The survey results highlighted common challenges faced by mechanization service providers in Ethiopia, including the unavailability of spare parts, financial constraints, limited maintenance services, and high fuel costs.

    Business Model Development

    As part of our transformative journey, we organized a workshop aimed at co-creating a business model for agricultural mechanization.

    During the workshop, we provided comprehensive training on the business model canvas, empowering participants with the tools and knowledge needed to develop innovative and viable strategies.

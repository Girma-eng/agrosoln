The Context

The Challenge

Our current food system is broken - the way we produce, distribute and consume food isn't working effectively.

Stem from deep, interconnected issues: historical, cultural, and global, contributing to environmental degradation, inequality, and food insecurity, failing both people and the planet.

By focusing on short-term gains rather than long-term sustainability, this approach results in soil depletion, biodiversity loss, and increased climate vulnerability.

The Gaps

Mainly face three intertwined challenges that must be addressed to unlock their potential.

Productivity Gap

Current output falls 30-50% below potential due to input limitations, limited mechanization and suboptimal farming practices.

Supply Gap

Challenges accessing inputs and reliable markets.

Skills Gap

Only 18% of farmers receive training, limiting adoption of modern techniques.

What We Strive For?

We envision a world where rural communities achieve true food sovereignty and independence by fully transitioning to regenerative lifestyles and systems - ensuring their resilience and empowering them to prosper for generations to come.

    • About Us

    Farm Hub Ethiopia is an agricultural company established in 2023 with a core focus on improving crop production practices and empowering smallholder farmers in Ethiopia. With a commitment to sustainability, innovation, and social impact, we have been actively engaged in providing agricultural solutions and services tailored to the needs of smallholder farmers. As the hands-on implementation partner in the agricultural ecosystem, we translate innovations from research labs (like Mission Labs) into practical, farmer-ready solutions.

    Our Role in the System:

    We are the implementation enginedelivering tools, training, and technologies directly to farmers' fields.

    Challenges Farm Hub Addresses

    Low Mechanization

    Less than 1% of Ethiopian farms use tractors, with 85% of land tilled by hand, severely limiting productivity.

    Fragmented Supply Chains

    Critical inputs and equipment often don't reach smallholder farmers due to weak distribution networks and high logistics costs.

    Knowledge Gaps

    Lack of access to training on machinery use and maintenance, reducing adoption rates. Lack of knowledge on agronomic practices and sustainability.

    The Mechanization Imperative

    Ethiopia's farm power availability is just 0.05 kW/ha (vs. 0.5+ kW/ha in emerging economies). Farm Hub's short-term focus on scalable mechanization solutions (pay-per-use models) can immediately boost productivity by 3-5x while solving affordability barriers.

    Our Vision

    Our vision is to revolutionize the agricultural sector by empowering farmers with innovative agricultural machinery, inputs, and sustainable practices, creating a future where Ethiopian agriculture thrives.

    Our Mission

    Through leveraging education, technology transfer, innovation and leadership development - our mission is to create a ripple effect of positive change, enabling rural communities to not only adapt but to lead in shaping a sustainable and resilient future.

    Our Achievements

    Learning Visit

    As part of our learning journey, we had the privilege of visiting UWEPO Farm in Uganda, owned by Nick Dekoning. The farm is a shining example of regenerative agriculture, showcasing excellence in sustainable and climate-resilient farming practices. We also visited Wondo Genet College and the Wondo Genet Research Center in Ethiopia, alongside our partners from Studio Soal (Ireland). This experience allowed us to explore innovations in agroforestry and herbal plant production, expanding our understanding of integrated, nature-based farming systems.

    Understanding the Existing System

    The survey was conducted to analyze the current mechanization landscape in Ethiopia and identify existing gaps. Our team thoroughly analyzed and documented the existing systems architecture, identifying key areas for improvement. It assessed key components such as available equipment, infrastructure, personnel, business models, and training facilities of mechanization service providers across various regions. This deep understanding allowed us to optimize processes and implement more effective solutions tailored to our specific needs.

    Mechanization Business Development

    As part of our transformative efforts, we organized a dedicated workshop focused on co-developing sustainable business models for agricultural mechanization. The session included hands-on training on the Business Model Canvas, equipping participants with practical tools and insights to design innovative, viable, and context-specific strategies for mechanization service delivery.

    Farm Hub Piloting

    Innovative agricultural mechanization solutions for Ethiopia

    Technology Sourcing

    Farm Lab Ethiopia conducted comprehensive technology sourcing to identify and import appropriate agricultural machinery from India. This included 12 different implements designed for Ethiopian farming conditions, paired with a 50hp tractor to serve as a complete mechanization solution.

    Partnership and Team Formation

    We established strategic partnerships with key stakeholders including Hawassa University, GFA/GIZ, and local service providers. Our team of experts worked collaboratively to implement and evaluate the Farm Hub model as a one-stop-shop for mechanization services.

    Field Trials & Evaluations

    Farm Lab conducted four rigorous field trials at IoT campus and external locations to evaluate equipment performance, seed rates, field capacity, and adaptability for new crops like soybean. These trials provided critical data on the effectiveness of different planting technologies under Ethiopian conditions.

    Demonstration Activities

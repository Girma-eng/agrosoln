01-The Context

Smallholder Farmers are the Front Line

Smallholder farmers are the foundation of Africa's food system yet they're the least supported. They're expected to grow food for the continent with no infrastructure, no finance, no market visibility, and no reliable services.

This situation results from structural problems that create a vicious cycle of challenges for smallholder farmers across Africa.
  • Manual farming Chronic low productivity
  • Informal markets Volatile, unstable prices
  • No access to finance No capacity to upgrade or grow
  • Highly climate-exposed No safety buffers
If we can't support the needs at the base of the system, nothing above it will function.

01-The Context

The Ecosystem struggles to support the Farmers

The supply chain of actors meant to support these farmers mechanisation service providers, input distributors, aggregators, buyers are fragmented, undercapitalised, and operating without standardisation or scale.

  • Services are fragmented Primarily informal and often donor-dependent with no sustainable business models
  • No interoperable platform Lack of standard business models prevents scaling across regions
  • No risk pooling No systemic way to share risk or create collective infrastructure
  • Ad hoc markets Spotty market access and inconsistent aggregation systems
This service ecosystem is the engine room for scale but everyone is building in isolation no compounding value.

01-The Context

Government struggles to support the Ecosystem

Governments are expected to deliver food security, rural job creation, and climate resilience. But they're trapped managing an informal sector that's hard to track, fund, or govern.

  • Inefficient investment Most public programs lack durability and fail to create systemic change
  • Siloed data Can't design or adapt policy fast enough without integrated systems
  • Foreign input dependence Over-reliance on imports leads to loss of food sovereignty
  • Pilot overload Too many isolated initiatives, not enough systemic solutions
Governments need investment-ready systems they can trust and align with not more one-off projects.

02 The System Vision

The Farmhub Model

An Integrated Stack for Agri-System Transformation

To build this ecosystem, we've defined three foundational components of a new integrated model we call the Farmhub Model:

Level 3: The Farmhub Mission Control

Level 2: The Farmhub Ops Centre

Level 1: The Farmhub Field Station

The deeper social and ecological impact required is not possible without each of these critical enabling layers.

02 The System Vision

Level 1: Farmhub Field Station

Structuring & Servicing the Last Mile

A Farmhub Field Station is a local service hub that provides smallholder farmers with access to the core infrastructure they need to close the yield, income, and market gap.

Each hub is a physical location franchised and locally operated offering affordable, standardised access to:
  • Mechanisation-as-a-service (e.g. tractor hire)
  • Input provisioning (seed, fertiliser, tools)
  • On-site storage and aggregation
  • Soil testing and basic diagnostics
  • Digital solutions for farm management
  • Farmer training and data collection
  • Value addition services
  • Logistics coordination
The Farmhub is the rural service hub for smallholder agriculture like a franchise field station that delivers yield, tools, and trust

02 The System Vision

Level 2: Farmhub Ops Centre

Coordinating Regional Operations

Farmhub Mission Control is the master franchise entity that oversees, enables, and standardises a network of Farmhub Field Stations across a region or country.

It leverages our Farmhub Playbook and acts as the coordination layer providing:
  • Standardised operating protocols
  • Franchise support and compliance
  • Logistics coordination
  • Regional marketing
  • Performance monitoring
  • Policy engagement
It's the operating layer that makes a scalable, coordinated, and financially viable Farmhub ecosystem possible.

02 The System Vision

Level 3: Farmhub Mission Control

Enabling System-wide Standardisation

Farmhub Infrastructure is the platform and backbone of the Farmhub system that manages shared, high-cost assets and protocols required for ecosystem scaling.

It does not operate Farmhubs it enables others to operate through:
  • Capital-intensive assets via PAYG
  • Farmhub OS digital platform
  • Governance infrastructure
  • Performance data systems
  • Financial visibility tools
  • Investment structuring
Think of it as the infrastructure grid, software stack, and financial rail combined built to serve distributed, regenerative rural economies.

    • ABOUT US

    Farm Hub Ethiopia is an agricultural company established in 2023 with a core focus on improving crop production and empowering smallholder farmers. With a commitment to innovation and sustainability, we have been actively engaged in providing agricultural solutions and services tailored to the needs of smallholder farmers. As the hands-on implementation partner in the agricultural ecosystem, we translate innovations from research labs (like Mission Labs) into practical, farmer-ready solutions.

    Our Role in the System:

    We are the implementation enginedelivering technologies, inputs and training directly to farmers' fields.

    Challenges Farm Hub Addresses

    Low Mechanization

    Less than 1% of Ethiopian farms use tractors, with 85% of land tilled by hand, severely limiting productivity.

    Fragmented Supply Chains

    Critical inputs and equipment often don't reach smallholder farmers due to weak distribution networks and high logistics costs.

    Knowledge Gaps

    Lack of access to training on machinery use and maintenance, reducing adoption rates. Lack of knowledge on agronomic practices and sustainability.

    The Mechanization Imperative

    Farm power availability is just 0.05 kW/ha (vs. 0.5+ kW/ha in emerging economies). Farm Hub's short-term focus on scalable mechanization solutions can immediately boost productivity by 30%-50%x while solving affordability barriers.
